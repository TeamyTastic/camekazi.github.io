The Diagnostic Stack

An integration of Snowden, Claydon, and The Ready into a layered diagnostic toolkit for organizational sensemaking.

Not convergence. Complementarity with acknowledged tensions.

Four Layers · Different Questions · Different Tools
Layer 01 — Epistemological

Cynefin / Snowden

"What kind of problem is this?"
systems-thinking-agent L5 polarity-mapping perspective-challenger

Domain classification before intervention. Clear, Complicated, Complex, Chaotic, or Confused? Match your approach to the domain. Don't apply ordered thinking to complex problems.

Tension: Design vs. Emergence
Snowden: Complex systems can't be pre-designed. Probe and respond.
Claydon: You CAN design architecture that hosts emergence. Boundaries + feedback loops + bounded experiments.
This tension is productive, not contradictory.
Layer 02 — Structural

Claydon / org-coherence

"How does architecture produce dysfunction?"
org-coherence systems-thinking-agent L4 immunity-to-change-coach

Run/Serve/Change load balance. Axion coherence. Confusion Tax. Six Survival Characters. Bands of Consequence. Five Estates. The architecture reveals why people behave as they do. Coherence is a design choice, not a personality trait.

Tension: Expert Interpretation vs. Distributed Sensemaking
Claydon: Expert reads transcripts, applies framework, diagnoses architecture.
Snowden: People IN the system should triangulate meaning, not an outside analyst.
The Ready: Practitioners need actionable interventions by Monday morning.
Layer 03 — Diagnostic

Depthfinding / The Ready

"What layer of organizational reality is the problem in?"
depthfinding systems-thinking-agent L2-L3 transcript-analysis ona

Sky (external forces) → Sunshine (stated strategy) → Twilight (actual operations) → Midnight (deep assumptions). You can only edit reality through the Twilight Zone. Find where strategy meets practice and intervene there.

Tension: Editable Scope
The Ready: Only edit the Twilight Zone (operational how).
Claydon: Redesign architecture at Band 4-5 (structural coherence, moral logic).
Can you redesign deep architecture, or only patch operations?
Layer 04 — Operational

Work-Mode Design

"How should time and space match cognitive demands?"
work-mode-design meeting-prep trickster-move liberating-structures

Four work modes (deep, shallow, collaborative, developmental) matched to four environments (home, office, third spaces, digital). Rhythm design, not rules. Where architecture meets the body. From Claydon's "What Work Belongs Where?" (2026).

The Trickster Move

"Don't fight the dashboard.
Change the meeting that worships it."

RTO Mandate

"4 days in office for culture"

→ Replace with 2 collaboration days with real agendas. Let deep work happen wherever it happens best.

Status Meeting

"Monday 9am all-hands, 45 minutes"

→ Async written updates (5 min each). Use reclaimed time for one real decision that needs the room.

Strategy Deck

"60-slide quarterly presentation"

→ One page: "What are we betting on? What are we stopping? What surprised us?" Discussion, not theatre.
