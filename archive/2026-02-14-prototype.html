02

Run, Serve, Change

Every organisation carries three kinds of work simultaneously. Not as departments or projects — as loads that sit on every role, at every level. When the architecture doesn't make these loads visible and governable, they leak into heroics, invisible glue work, and evening emails. That's where the characters come from.
Run

Keep it going

Keeping the current system operational.
Execution of existing processes, meeting regulatory requirements, delivering today's work, managing P&L, handling customer service and compliance. The work the system is designed to do.
Daily/weekly cycles · Process execution · Reporting · Compliance · Customer delivery · Firefighting
Serve

Stitch it together

Horizontal integration across silos.
Connecting markets and functions, standardising practices, sharing innovations, reducing duplication, translating global directives into local reality. The invisible glue work that keeps a complex organisation from fragmenting.
Cross-functional coordination · Relationship repair · Translation · De-escalation · Smoothing handovers · "Human middleware"
Change

Redesign it

Altering the system itself.
Redesigning structures, practices, and constraints. Experimenting with new models. Deciding what legacy processes die. Shifting organisational architecture. The work that makes the future different from the present.
Experimentation · Architecture redesign · New capabilities · Killing legacy · Sponsoring safe-to-fail probes

The Fractal Pattern

Every level carries all three loads — but the proportions shift. The higher the role, the more of its value should come from development, not just delivery. When everyone operates at 80–90% Run, there is no structural carrier for Serve or Change.

When the Load Is Misallocated

Different misallocations produce different character distributions.

Run dominance everywhere

Everyone at 80–90% Run. Prevents systemic learning. Burns out complexity carriers. Serve and Change work done invisibly in evenings — or not at all.

Produces → Exhausted, Confused, Heroes burning out

Unrecognised Serve work

People solving cross-functional problems informally receive no credit. The system can't replicate their solutions. Their work is invisible to the performance regime.

Produces → Exhausted (carrying invisible load), Role-Actors (learning not to bother)

Change without support

Experiments fail silently. No pathway from "local hack" to "regional pattern" to "standard." Innovation becomes theatre because there's no carrier to move it.

Produces → Heroes (pushing change by willpower), Game-Players (narrating change without doing it)

All three dumped on same people

Run the operational load, Serve everyone by absorbing coordination, AND Change by trying to correct the system. That's not leadership development — that's a recipe for Stretch → Tangle → Break.

Produces → Heroes collapsing into Exhausted

03

Organisational Physics

Three structural conditions manufacture the characters. Different answers produce different casts — and predict the plot.

Structural Load

Where does Run, Serve, Change actually sit?

Beyond the org chart — who is actually carrying operational delivery, relationship maintenance, and transformation work? Where is it leaking into heroics and invisible glue? Redraw the org in three colours: estimate actual weekly percentages for each person.

Regulatory Grammar

What gets rewarded — truth, theatre, caution, speed, obedience?

The real incentive architecture. Not what the values deck says — what behaviour actually gets promoted, punished, and ignored. Bell curves that force a fixed percentage into "needs improvement." Promotion criteria that reward narrow visibility over systemic impact.

Moral Universe

Who benefits when the system "performs well"? Who absorbs harm?

Every organisation has a moral project, spoken or not. When the system runs "as designed," whose upside flows to one layer? Whose downside absorbs the strain? Who carries the hidden complexity — and at what personal cost?

05

Warning Signs by Character Distribution

Count the characters in your system. The dominant cast tells you the plot.

Heroes proliferating

System rewarding truth-telling but not supporting it. Heroes burn bright — then burn out.

Role-Actors dominant

Appearance over reality in the regulatory grammar. The system is being held together by invisible translation labour.

Exhausted everywhere

Invisible Serve work not recognised. Load is being outsourced into nervous systems. The system's truth sensors are going quiet.

No Ironists remaining

System has expelled or exhausted its redesigners. Nobody left who can see the theatre AND change the carriers.

Game-Players rising

Legitimacy signals divorced from reality. The system is selecting for performance of competence over actual competence.

Confused people everywhere

Incoherent grammar — contradictory demands without resolution. Strategy isn't being implemented, it's being locally survived.
