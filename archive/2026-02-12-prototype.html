Mob Programming

Whole team. Same thing. Same time. Same computer. With AI. A structured ritual for shared understanding.

"For an idea to go from your head into the computer, it MUST go through someone else's hands."
Llewellyn Falco — The foundational constraint that makes mob programming a learning activity, not just a coding activity.

Evidence from the field

Crisp (Sweden) — Daily 3hr sessions

AI as "superintelligent advisor — always available, never tired." Eliminated code reviews, removed key-person dependencies, reduced technical debt.

"We tried splitting up to hit a deadline, but reintegration was painful. We're faster together."

Atlassian — Define → Spec → Generate → Review → Iterate

2–4 developers with rotating facilitator. Features in 1–2 days vs typical week. AI draft accuracy: ~50% → ~80% by project end.

Roles

5 roles for AI-collaborative sessions. Minimum 2 humans — AI augments, doesn't replace the social learning.

AI-adapted roles

Driver
Types prompts, presses buttons. Does NOT decide what to create — only implements what the Navigator describes.
Human only
Facilitator
Runs timer, tracks rotation, calls breaks. Key question: "Are we stuck?"
AI: partial
Subject Matter Expert
Brings business context the AI lacks. Domain knowledge, user stories, edge cases.
Human only
Red Team
Critiques output, pokes holes, challenges assumptions. "What could go wrong?"
AI: yes
Outcome Keeper
Stays on mission, prevents AI tangents. "Is this still solving our problem?"
AI: partial

Experiment Designer

Build a hypothesis, run the safe-to-try check, decide whether to proceed.

"Do a radical thing at a non-radical scale."
Aaron Dignan, The Ready — Don't create a 1-hour meeting-free zone. Eliminate all meetings for 2 weeks. Experience the change fully before judging it.

Kamer's 8 tips for experiments

#TipApplied to mob programming
1The test comes from you — genuine passion, not assignedChampion volunteers, don't conscript
2Define the desired effect — concrete, measurable"Resolve first ticket in <2 hours"
3Be bold — radical thing, non-radical scaleFull mob week, not a timid 1-hour trial
4One thing at a time — change one variableMob OR new AI tool. Not both simultaneously
5Right time horizon — 8 weeks ideal, 16 max2–3 sprint cycles to build real skill
6Don't kill it early — persist past discomfort3 sessions is too few. Commit to 8+
7Retrospect regularly — monthly learningsEnd-of-session retro + monthly pattern review
8Expect frustration — 2 steps forward, 1 back"Why is this slower?" is normal at first

The T-shape model

From Rodney Evans at The Ready: teach one universal practice across all teams (the bar of the T — e.g. retrospectives). Let a smaller adventurous group experiment widely (the stem of the T). That pioneering group becomes the organizational model.

"Start with an adventurous group already doing things differently — not a group stuck in dysfunction. It will be more inspiring."Jurriaan Kamer, The Ready

Mob Programming RPG

Willem Larsen's gamified mob experience. Earn XP by embodying role behaviours. Print the cards, bring to your next session.

Level 1 — Core

Level 1
Driver
Ask clarifying questions about typing. Use new keyboard shortcuts. Type something you disagree with. Ignore non-Navigator instructions.
Level 1
Navigator
Request ideas from the mob. Filter suggestions and direct Driver precisely. Communicate high-level intent. Follow TDD cycle.
Level 1
Mobber
Yield to less-privileged voices. Contribute ideas. Ask questions until understanding is reached. Listen attentively.

Level 2 — Support

Level 2
Researcher
Share relevant documentation. Find blog insights. Locate coding forum solutions.
Level 2
Sponsor
Amplify underheard perspectives. Support least-privileged contributors. Recognise moments of excellence.
Level 2
Rear Admiral
Offer Navigator guidance privately. Provide minimal cues. Guide at highest abstraction level.

Level 3 — Quality

Level 3
Automationist
Identify repeated tool tasks. Notice repeated processes. Recognise boilerplate. Suggest automation.
Level 3
Nose
Flag long lines, complex conditionals, duplication. Name unnamed things. Propose improvements.
Level 3
Archivist
Record alternatives visibly. Document ideas on whiteboard. Display current task. Capture decisions.

Level 4 — Culture

Level 4
Conductor
Combine ideas (discard the first). Elicit quiet members. Make ridiculous proposals. Defer to junior voices.
Level 4
Traffic Cop
Propose new agreements. Alert to process violations. Support adherence. Identify outdated agreements.
Level 4
Dr. Feel Good
Express appreciation (SBI model). Share joy. Acknowledge happiness. Retrospect positive moments.
Level 4
Major Pain
Articulate difficulties. Demonstrate empathy. Surface ignored pain points. Call retrospectives on pain.

First rule: Treat each other with Kindness, Consideration, and Respect at all times.
